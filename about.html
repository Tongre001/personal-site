Values

Both of my parents are immigrants from Mexico and they value hard work and education.

Learn from others to do it yourself

My mom never finished elementary school, because she chose to work at a very young age to help her grandfather around the house. Her father worked in the vineyards in Napa and saved up until he was able to bring his 9 children over to the US. All of my aunts and uncles now work and live near or in Calistoga, but each of them always encouraged me to be good in school and get an education. My uncles always enjoyed doing carpentry and speaking truthfully, I never enjoyed it, however, the results were always spectacular. The one thing that they always said that stuck to me was that even thoughI did not want to do the work, I should suck it up and learn because you never know when it is going to come in handy. Instead of calling someone to do it for you, you can save a buck or two knowing how to do it yourself.

Education and Hardwork

My father was on his last year of high-school when he decided to migrate over to the US for a better life. He is very proud of his independence and his hard work. He became a contractor and he made me go to work with him (sometimes) when I was very young. I remember playing with the rubble sometimes when he would break down the walls. Then, when I was 12, he made me start working with him, which I greatly valued. He took me to many houses during the year and it made me dream that one day I could live in one of those houses and he said that one day I can but only if I work hard for them. That wasn't the only lesson I got from him though. At some point I dreaded working with him, because the work was so heavy; having to carry 65 pound buckets up the ladder, carry 80 pound sacks of cement, and having to set up scaffolding 2 stories high. By the time I was 18 it was the only thing I knew how to work in and my dad kept saying that if I didn't go to school I might end up like him. I am not complaining, all I am saying is that it scared me, and I knew that I didn't want to do it all of my life. From working with him, I learned two things: not take things for granted and that education would bring to me an enjoyable job.
